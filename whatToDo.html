Family:

If you are coming with your family don't worry, you can find activities for all ages and preferences. Dublin has a vast number of parks for a sunny (and rare) days:

  • Stephen's Green: Stephen's Green: right in the end of Grafton Street and beside Stephen's Green Shopping Center, this park is a "must go" place in Dublin, as is in the middle of everything it's busy at anytime at the day, but you always can find I spot to relax, listening to the city's sounds, the bunkers playing nearby, watch the people coming and going.
  • Phoenix Park + Dublin Zoo: It’s the biggie. At 1,750 acres, Phoenix Park is Europe’s largest urban park. It's the official residence of the President of Ireland and the US ambassador, and is the best place to escape the capital’s bustling streets. Among expansive fields and monuments like the Papal Cross, the Wellington Monument and Ashtown Castle, there are plenty of places to explore, if you're lucky you mind find the residentes, the deers. There you can also find the Dublin Zoo, that is one of the oldest in the world, very well maintained with a lot of aticities for kids.
  • Botanic Park: Based on the northside of Dublin, it's absolute gourgeous, with greenhouses, 1.500 species of plants from everyday to exotics. Twice a day it's possible to join a guided tour.
  • St. Anne's Park: Up to the northside, opposite to the Bull Island, you can find there a classical Chinese Garden, a Herculanean Temple and a clock.
  • People’s Park: If you want to run way from the busy an noisy city center you can't scape to the southside of Dublin, Dun Laoghaire is a very charming area, you can go there by bus or dart, just a couple of minutes walk you'll arrive at the park, with a lovely sea view, playground and a cafe, at the weekends there's a lovely markert with food and more.

You can also explore some of the many museums in the capital:

  • EPIC Museum: A big emphasis to this place, a mandatory stop to understand a part of the Irish history and the big emigration, the museum is divided in twenty galleries indiviadully themed, you'll get into a time machine and immerse into the history.
  • National Gallery of Ireland: Is right in the city center, one of the entrances is on the Merrion Square, another nice spot to stop by, The gallery has an extensive, representative collection of Irish paintings and is also notable for its Italian Baroque and Dutch masters painting. But more than that, the museum is based in such a lovely place, well organised and is divided in sections and each one has bright and colerfull walls.
  • The Museum of Natural History: The kids will love it! Also based at Merrion Square, The Natural History Collection comprises over 2 million items, in the fields of zoology and geology; a million of the specimens being insects.

Irish Culture

The culture of Ireland includes it's traditions and customs, as well as folklore, music, language, art, and food. The Gaelic culture originated in Ireland but eventually expanded to Scotland, and much of northwestern Europe. The Irish culture has been influenced by Scottish, English, and Anglo-Norman cultures throughout history. Today there are cultural differences between the Protestant and Catholic Irish, and many Irish traditions have become wn around the world, including Halloween and Saint Patrick's Day. Archaeological evidence has shown that farming in Ireland began about the time humans first began to settle.

Ireland has the highest number of red-haired people per capita in the world. Over 10% of the population has red hair in Ireland. This is a lot higher than any other countries although the UK has a high percentage as well.

Ireland is the first country in the world that legalised same-sex marriage by referendum, in 2017, Ireland became the 20th country in the world to allow same-sex marriage.

The President of Ireland has little to no power, Ireland’s power is not in the hands of the president, the role is mainly ceremonial and symbolic. The person who really has the power is the head of government, also referred to as “The Taoiseach”.
