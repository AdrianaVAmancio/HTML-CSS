Dublin bus:Buses serve all areas of Central Dublin, and many suburbs in the Greater Dublin Area too. The best way to plan a journey by bus is by using Transport for Irelandâ€™s journey planner or the Moovit service. Both tools allow you to enter a start point and end point for your journey and will then provide you with a map of the route for your journey along with operator and departure information. It's also import that you know that to pay on the bus you need to have coins or a leap card, check more informations at Dublin bus website.

Luas:Luas are the tram network, there are two lines, Green and Red, which serve many areas of Central Dublin and some residential areas (mainly in the south and southwest of the city). On Luas you can only pay by leapcard, you have to validate the card on the way in and out, not doing that can result in fines, check more informations at Luas website. The map shows you the routes:

Dart:In Dublin, the electric rail system called the DART (Dublin Area Rapid Transit) is a quick and easy way to get around Dublin. It runs along the coast of the Irish Sea from Malahide or Howth in north County Dublin southwards as far as Greystones, Co Wicklow.
